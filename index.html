📖 What is Prompt Engineering?

The skill of talking to AI

A prompt is what you type into an AI. Prompt engineering is the art of writing prompts that get you amazing, useful results — instead of boring or wrong ones. Think of it like learning a superpower: the same AI gives completely different answers depending on how you ask!

⚡ The 5 Golden Rules

Rule 1: Be Specific

Vague questions get vague answers. The more detail you give, the better.

❌ Weak Prompt

Tell me about space.

✅ Strong Prompt

Explain how black holes form in 3 bullet points. Use an analogy a 13-year-old would understand.

Rule 2: Give it a Role

Tell the AI who to be. "Act as a..." changes everything about tone, depth, and style.

❌ Weak Prompt

Explain photosynthesis.

✅ Strong Prompt

You are an enthusiastic science teacher. Explain photosynthesis to a 13-year-old student who loves video games. Use gaming analogies.

Rule 3: Specify the Format

Tell AI exactly how you want the output — bullet points, a story, a table, a poem, step-by-step.

❌ Weak Prompt

What should I study for my math test?

✅ Strong Prompt

Create a 7-day study schedule for a middle school math test on fractions and decimals. Format it as a table with: Day | Topic | Time (mins) | Practice activity.

Rule 4: Give Examples (Few-Shot)

Show the AI a sample of what you want, then ask it to do more like that.

❌ Weak Prompt

Write a fun AI fact.

✅ Strong Prompt

Here is an example of the style I want: "Netflix's AI watches your eyes to see when you pause — and learns your taste from that!" Write 3 more wild AI facts in exactly this style: one sentence, starts with the company name, ends with an exclamation.

Rule 5: Use Chain-of-Thought

Add "think step by step" or "explain your reasoning" — it makes AI smarter and more accurate.

❌ Weak Prompt

Is it better to study in the morning or at night?

✅ Strong Prompt

Think step by step: Is it better for a middle schooler to study in the morning or at night? Consider: memory science, sleep, focus, and school schedules. Then give a final recommendation with 2 action tips.

🚀 Advanced Techniques

Zero-Shot vs Few-Shot Prompting

Zero-shot: Just ask, no examples. Works for simple tasks.
Few-shot: Give 2-3 examples first, then ask. Way better for creative or specific tasks.

Temperature & Tone Control

Words like "be creative and unexpected" or "be precise and factual" literally change how the AI thinks. You are controlling its "temperature" — how wild vs. careful it is.

Constraint Prompting

Add rules and limits to force better outputs: "in exactly 3 sentences", "only use words a 10-year-old knows", "no bullet points", "under 100 words".

✅ Example

Explain how WiFi works. Rules: exactly 3 sentences, no technical jargon, must include one funny analogy.

Iterative Prompting

Great prompts are not written once — you refine them! After each reply, say "make it more funny", "shorter", "add an example", "explain the 3rd point more." The back-and-forth IS the skill.
